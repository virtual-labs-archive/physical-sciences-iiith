Familiarization with DIPOLE MOMENT.

The molar polarization can be calculated from the dielectric constant through the Clausius-Mossotti equation:
                             P=(κ -1)/(κ+2)[(M/ρ)]
where M is the molar mass and ρ is the mass density of the dielectric material. The dielectric constant is unitless, and so the molar polarization has units of cm3/mol.
The dielectric constant at these high frequencies is related to the refractive index by the simple formula: κ = n2 where n is the refractive index.
The contribution from induced dipoles is given by Lorentz-Lorenz equation:
Pi=(M/ρ )[(n2-1)/(n2+2)]
The distortion polarizability (α) is defined as:
(n2-1)/(n2+2)=(4π /3)NAα
Thus we can write the total molar polarization as:
P=(4π /3)NA(α + μ2/3kT)
where μ is the permanent dipole moment of a single molecule, k is the Boltzmann's constant and T is the temperature in Kelvin. Thus a if P is plotted against 1/T it should give a straight line whose slope will give us the dipole moment and the intercept will give us polarizability.
